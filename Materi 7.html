Teorema Fundamental Homomorfisma Grup (TUHG)

Teorema

Jika \(f:G\to S\) adalah homomorfisma grup, maka berlaku

\[G/Ker(f) \cong Im(f).\]

Diperhatikan homomorfisma $\beta: \mathbb{Z}\to\mathbb{Z}_5$, yakni dengan definisi $\beta(n)=\overline{n}$ untuk setiap $n \in \mathbb{Z}.$ Mudah dipahami bahwa $\beta$ bersifat surjektif dan juga mudah diketahui bahwa $Ker(f)= 5\mathbb{Z}.$

Berdasarkan TFHG, diperoleh \(\mathbb{Z}/5\mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}_5.\)

How to show two groups are isomorphic

The standard way to show \(G \cong H\) is to construct an isomorphism \(\phi: G \to H\). When the domain is a quotient, there is another method, due to the Fundamental Homomorphism Theorem.

Suppose we want to show that \(G/N \cong H.\) There are two approaches:

  1. Define a map \(\phi: G/N \to H\) and prove that it is well-defined, a homomorphism, and a bijection.
  2. Define a map \(\phi : G \to H\) and prove that it is a homomorphism, a surjection (onto), and that \(Ker(\phi)=N\).
